Research

My research interest is focused around jets from actively accreting supermassive black holes - or Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN). Originally being interested in the effect on star formation, my research interest has now evolved to look at kpc-scale 'wings' that appear in some jetted sources.

I am part of the Black Hole group, in the astrophysics department at the University of Bristol, which is lead by Andy Young.

I work mainly with X-ray and LOFAR (LOw Frequency ARray) observations and have joined the LOFAR-VLBI (Very Long Baseline Interferometery) collaboration.

Academic

I currently work on active galaxies, specifically FRII Quasars with strong X-ray emission. I work with Chandra (X-ray), HST (optical) and VLA & LOFAR (Radio) using multiwavelength data to investigate emission mechanisms of active galaxies to understand the ongoing processes. Recently I have gained an interest in Galaxies that have a distinct 'X'-shape in X-ray emission and LOFAR emission, such that there is emission near-prependicular to the jet axis.
